Your Competitor Just Set Up an Automated Follow-Up System. Here's Exactly How It Works.

At Pulseforge, we build marketing automation systems for small business owners in Manchester, NH who are done spending their evenings manually sending follow-up emails and appointment reminders. One question we hear constantly is: "What does this actually look like day to day?" So here's a plain-English breakdown of what a real system does once it's running.

The Trigger Is Everything

Automation doesn't mean "send emails randomly." It means every action a lead takes triggers the right response at the right time. Someone fills out your contact form at 9pm? They get a personalized reply in under two minutes, not the next morning when you remember to check. Someone books an appointment and then ghosts the confirmation? The system sends a reminder, then a follow-up, then a last-chance nudge, without you touching anything. The trigger is what separates a system from a newsletter blast.

The Sequences That Actually Convert

There are three workflows we see do the heaviest lifting for businesses in Manchester and the surrounding area.

The first is the new lead sequence. A prospect reaches out, they get an immediate acknowledgment, a value-driven follow-up 24 hours later, and a soft close on day three. That alone recovers leads that would've gone cold while you were out running a job.

The second is the no-show reactivation sequence. Someone books, doesn't show, and never hears from you again? That's a solvable problem. A two-step automated message brings a meaningful percentage of those contacts back to the calendar without a single manual touchpoint.

The third is the review request sequence. After a completed job or appointment, a timed message goes out asking for a Google review. Businesses in Manchester that run this consistently see their review counts climb within 60 days, which directly affects how they rank in local search.

What You Actually Have to Do

This is the part people are surprised by. Once the system is built and connected to your existing tools, your job is to check the dashboard, handle the replies that need a human touch, and let the rest run. You're not writing the same email for the fourteenth time. You're not wondering if you remembered to follow up with that lead from Tuesday. The system holds all of that.

It's not magic. It's just structure, applied consistently, which is exactly what most overwhelmed business owners can't find the time to build on their own.
