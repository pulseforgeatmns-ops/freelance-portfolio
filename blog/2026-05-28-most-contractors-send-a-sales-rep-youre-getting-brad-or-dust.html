Most Contractors Send a Sales Rep. You're Getting Brad or Dustin.

That distinction matters more than homeowners usually realize until they've already signed a contract with someone else.

When you call Mountain State Home Innovations for a free estimate, you're not getting a middleman who walks your property, writes up a number, and hands the job off to a crew you've never met. You're getting one of the two guys who will actually do the work — Brad or Dustin — walking your yard, looking at your siding or your deck framing or your windows, and talking through what they're seeing in plain language.

Here's what that actually looks like.

What Happens During the Walkthrough

Brad or Dustin comes out to your property — whether you're in Charleston, Hurricane, Huntington, or somewhere in between across Kanawha, Putnam, or Cabell County — and takes a close look at the area you're concerned about.

That means getting eyes on things most homeowners don't think to check: the framing behind a deck ledger, the condition of trim boards underneath siding, the way water is running off a roofline toward a window. It's not a clipboard-and-photograph visit. It's a working conversation about what's going on with your home.

If you're not sure what you need — maybe you know something's wrong but you're not sure if it's a repair or a full replacement — that walkthrough is where you figure it out. There's no pressure to decide anything on the spot, and no pitch for a service package you didn't ask about.

What You Can Expect from the Conversation

Some homeowners come in with a clear picture of the project. Others just know they've got a deck that doesn't feel solid anymore, or siding that's been taking on moisture, or windows that are letting in drafts they can feel through the glass in January.

Either way, Brad or Dustin will give you a straight answer about what they're looking at and what they'd recommend. If a repair makes more sense than a replacement, they'll say so. If there's something that needs to happen before winter hits hard in West Virginia, they'll point that out too.

Mountain State Home Innovations is licensed under WV065578, and as a locally owned operation, the owners are accountable to every job in a way a larger company simply isn't. You have direct access to Brad or Dustin from the first phone call through the last day on site.

Why This Matters Before You Commit

Getting an estimate is how you decide whether to move forward — but it's also how you decide who you're trusting with your home. A walkthrough with the actual person doing the work gives you a much clearer read on both of those questions at once.

You get to ask the real questions. What's the timeline? What's the process if something unexpected shows up once work starts? What does a finished deck or new siding installation actually look like on a house like mine?

Those conversations don't happen when you're talking to someone who won't be on your property past the estimate.
