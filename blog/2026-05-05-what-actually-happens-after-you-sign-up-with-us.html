What Actually Happens After You Sign Up With Us

Most marketing tools send you a welcome email and then leave you staring at a dashboard wondering what to do next. We built Pulseforge to work the other way around — you tell us about your business, and we figure out the rest.

Here's what that actually looks like behind the scenes.

The First 48 Hours Are the Busiest (For Us, Not You)

When a new client comes on — say, a plumber in Manchester, NH who's been writing his own follow-up texts at 10pm after long days on job sites — the first thing we do is sit down and map out what his customer journey actually looks like. Not a generic template. His version.

We're asking questions like: When does someone usually reach out? What do they need to hear before they book? What makes a customer refer a friend? From those answers, we build the message sequences, the timing, and the triggers that make the whole thing run.

By the time that plumber wakes up on day three, there's a working system ready to follow up with every new lead automatically. He didn't write a single word of it.

The Part Nobody Sees

A lot goes on in the background that clients never have to think about. We're testing subject lines, adjusting send times based on open rate data, and making sure nothing lands in a spam folder. If a message isn't getting responses, we swap it out. If something's working unusually well, we figure out why and apply it elsewhere.

We do this for small business owners across southern New Hampshire — landscapers, consultants, fitness studios, contractors — and the patterns we see across accounts actually make each individual setup smarter over time.

It's not magic. It's just paying close attention to the numbers so our clients don't have to.

Why We Work This Way

We're based in Manchester, NH, which means most of our clients are people we might run into at Strange Brew or the Saturday farmers market. That keeps us honest. When someone tells us they finally stopped dreading Monday mornings because their follow-ups just happen on their own, that means something to us.

We didn't build Pulseforge to sell software. We built it because small business owners are already wearing too many hats, and "marketing person" shouldn't have to be one of them.

If you're curious what a setup like this might look like for your business, we're happy to walk you through it — no pressure, just a real conversation about whether it's a good fit.
