About  ·  the operator and the agents

An operator directing agents.

Pulseforge is fifteen named software agents, one founder, and a handful of small businesses I work for. I run it from Manchester, New Hampshire, and clients hire the system by the month.

01  ·  What I do

Pulseforge is the AI marketing system I built for local service businesses: contractors, cleaners, restaurants, auto shops, med spas, fitness studios. The kind of operators who don't have a marketing department, don't want to learn five new tools, and need a system that runs without being managed.

I direct the work most agencies promise but few deliver: finding prospects, sending the right outreach, monitoring the replies, posting the content, watching the reviews, and reporting back every morning. The difference is how I do it. Fifteen specialized agents, each named, each with a defined role, and each accountable to a daily log, handle the work that two or three human employees would otherwise do.

This isn't a tool you use. It's a team of agents I direct.

02  ·  Founder

Jacob Maynard.

I spent more than a decade running restaurants and a small cleaning company before I built Pulseforge. That experience is the entire reason this agency exists.

Local service operators don't need another piece of software. They need someone who's been the one rotating the floor on a Saturday night, the one calling a no-show employee at 7am, the one who has to make payroll work with a two-week-out invoice. Most marketing agencies I encountered as an operator either didn't understand the work, didn't understand the margins, or sold me packages built for businesses ten times the size of mine.

Pulseforge is the agency I would have hired. I only work with local service businesses. I charge by the month, not the campaign. I deliver one digest a day with what the agents did, what came back, and what needs you. And I do the work, not the slide deck about the work.

I'm still based in Manchester, NH. I still bartend a few shifts a week. I built every agent that runs this agency, by hand, from scratch. That's not a brag. It's the model. The agency is the system, and the system is built by someone who actually knows what it's like to be the client.

04  ·  How I work

Four principles.

i.

Do the work.

I don't pitch decks, I ship systems. Every engagement starts with me building, not strategizing. Strategy without execution is what most agencies sell. I sell execution that already happened.

ii.

Report honestly.

Every morning at seven, every client gets a digest of what happened the day before: what worked, what didn't, and what needs their attention. Nothing inflated, nothing buried. If a week is slow, the digest says so.

iii.

Own the stack.

The agents are mine, the database is mine, and the deployment is mine. I don't resell another platform with a markup. When something needs to change, I change it that night, if it matters.

iv.

Stay local.

I work with local service businesses because I understand them. I'm based in Manchester. I bartend. I've run floors and managed crews. That operator background is the moat.
