02  ·  What happens next

It's a conversation, not a pitch.

We don't have a slide deck. We're not going to pressure-sell you. The fifteen minutes is exactly that — a real conversation where we ask about your business and tell you, plainly, whether our system fits.

About a third of the calls end with "this isn't the right fit right now." We'd rather tell you that early than waste your money for three months. The other two-thirds end with a setup conversation — and those clients are the ones we want to work with anyway.

04  ·  The call itself

Three things happen.

i.

We listen.

You walk us through your business, your offer, and what's working or not working in your current marketing. Maybe ten minutes of the fifteen.

ii.

We diagnose.

We tell you what we think — what would work, what wouldn't, which tier fits, and where the realistic upside is. If Pulseforge isn't the right answer, we say so.

iii.

You decide.

No pressure to sign on the call. You take a day or a week, talk it over, and decide. If yes, we send a Letter of Authorization and start onboarding.

Or just email.

If you'd rather skip the form and write a real email, that works too. Everything comes to the founder.
