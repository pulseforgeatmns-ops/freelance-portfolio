The build

Everything that was built, and how it works

This is not a template or a concept. Every component below was built, tested, and is ready to deploy for a restaurant using Toast POS. No ongoing work required on your end after setup.

Trigger

Toast POS webhook integration

The system activates automatically the moment a new customer completes their first transaction. No manual exports, no CSV uploads. The trigger fires from Toast directly.

Sequence

5-email loyalty welcome sequence

A fully written, timed email sequence delivered over the first 30 days. Each email has a specific purpose: welcome, soften, offer, remind, retain. Copy written for a local restaurant voice.

Incentive

Unique promo code generation

Each new customer receives a unique, trackable promo code on Day 1. No duplicate codes, no manual creation. Generated automatically at the moment the sequence begins.

Data

Google Sheets CRM logging

Every contact, send, and promo code is logged automatically to a Google Sheet you own and can view anytime. Full visibility into who entered the sequence and when.

The sequence

30 days. 5 emails. Fully automated.

Here is what goes out after a new customer visits your restaurant for the first time.

1

Day 1 — Welcome

Thank you and a reason to come back

Sent within hours of the first visit. Thanks them by name, delivers their unique promo code, and sets the tone for the relationship. Feels personal, sends automatically.

2

Day 4 — Soft touch

A reminder without the pressure

A short, conversational follow-up that mentions their promo is still waiting. Low-pressure tone. Keeps your name visible without feeling like a newsletter.

3

Day 8 — Social proof

What other customers are saying

Builds confidence in the decision to return. Can include a specific dish, a seasonal offer, or a community moment. Customized per restaurant.

4

Day 13 — Urgency

Promo code expiration notice

Lets the customer know their promo is expiring soon. Creates a genuine reason to act. Drives the second visit, which is the most important conversion in restaurant retention.

5

Day 30 — Close the loop

One last note, no pressure

A final short email that leaves the relationship open. No hard sell. Ends the sequence on a human note that a surprising number of customers respond to.
