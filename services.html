Services  ·  what the agency actually does

Four capabilities. One team.

Lead generation, outreach, operations, and content — handled by fifteen agents, reporting to one founder, working twenty-four hours a day. Here's what each part of the system does, and what you get.

01  ·  Lead generation

Pipeline that finds itself.

Every night, Scout combs search engine results, business directories, and Google Places for prospects matching your Ideal Customer Profile. Faye and Link enrich those with verified business emails, dedupe against your existing pipeline, and reject the junk before it ever reaches your CRM.

The result lands in your dashboard scored, sorted, and contactable — no spreadsheets, no manual research, no list- buying.

  • Nightly prospect sweeps  ·  up to six verticals, two regions, twenty to seventy prospects per run
  • Verified emails  ·  sourced via Hunter and Prospeo, validated, deliverable
  • ICP scoring  ·  every prospect rated 0–100 against your defined target
  • Junk rejection  ·  an aggressive validator filters out roughly forty percent of raw results
  • CSV + dashboard delivery  ·  or piped straight into your CRM

02  ·  Outreach

Outreach that answers itself.

Emmett runs a four-step cold email sequence pacing one business day apart, throttled to send only during the established window — Tuesday through Thursday, 9am to 2pm. Every send is human-paced, vertical-tuned, and capped per client to protect deliverability.

Riley reads every reply and routes it — warm leads to your setter calendar, objections to a response queue, unsubscribes to suppression. Nothing waits for a human until it matters. Cal places a follow-up voice call to warm replies within the hour, using a natural-sounding setter voice tuned to the vertical.

  • Four-step sequences  ·  Day 0, Day 4, Day 8, Day 13, tested cadence
  • Reply classification  ·  warm, objection, unsubscribe, out-of-office, routed automatically
  • Warm-signal flagging  ·  two opens in seven days promotes a prospect to setter follow-up
  • Voice setter calls  ·  placed within an hour of a warm reply, fully logged
  • Per-client send caps  ·  40 sends per day per client, protecting your sender reputation

03  ·  Operations

Reporting that briefs itself.

Max coordinates execution across the agency — making sure every agent runs when it should, escalating anomalies, keeping work moving across all clients. The agency's chief of staff, but software.

Pulse monitors the system three times daily — 9am, noon, 3pm — classifies the health of every agent as critical, warn, or ok, and only alerts when it's earned. Sam keeps the CRM in sync. Penny handles billing. Rex compiles a weekly recap of what happened across all clients. The system reports on itself.

  • Execution coordination  ·  Max keeps every agent on schedule, escalates anomalies, prevents work from falling through
  • Three health checks daily  ·  system-wide monitoring with alert classification
  • Weekly digest  ·  Rex compiles a Sunday recap of the week's activity across every client
  • CRM sync  ·  prospect status, engagement history, agent activity, all in one view
  • Stripe-native billing  ·  transparent monthly invoicing, no surprise fees

04  ·  Content

Content that compounds.

Paige publishes weekly across LinkedIn, Facebook, your company blog, and Google Business Profile — vertical-tuned content that reinforces your authority and feeds your SEO. Sketch generates the visuals. Vera monitors your Google reviews and surfaces new ones with context for response.

Every post is reviewed before publishing — you stay in the loop without writing the drafts. Over months, this is what compounds your local search ranking, your social proof, and your inbound flow.

  • Weekly blog post  ·  researched, drafted, and published to your site
  • LinkedIn + Facebook posts  ·  scheduled across the week through Buffer
  • Google Business Profile posts  ·  weekly, with photos when supplied
  • Custom visuals  ·  generated per post, matched to your brand
  • Review monitoring  ·  new Google reviews surfaced with sentiment and response suggestion
