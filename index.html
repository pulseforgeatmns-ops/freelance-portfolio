What I orchestrate  ·  the four-band system

Four capabilities. One operator.

Lead generation, outreach, operations, and content, handled by fifteen agents under my direction.

01

Pipeline that finds itself.

Scout combs search results, business directories, and Google Places nightly for prospects matching your Ideal Customer Profile. Faye and Link enrich and dedupe. Junk gets rejected before it touches your CRM.

Agents · Scout · Faye · Link
02

Outreach that answers itself.

Emmett runs four-step cold sequences vertical-tuned and send-window enforced. Riley reads replies and routes them: warm leads to the setter, objections to the queue, unsubscribes to suppression. Cal calls warm replies within the hour.

Agents · Emmett · Riley · Cal · Ivy
03

Reporting that briefs itself.

Max coordinates the system, escalating anomalies. Pulse monitors health three times daily. Rex compiles a weekly recap. You get a clear picture of what happened without managing the system that produces it.

Agents · Max · Pulse · Rex · Sam
04

Content that compounds.

Paige publishes weekly across LinkedIn, Facebook, your blog, and Google Business Profile. Sketch generates visuals. Vera monitors your reviews. Over months, your search authority, social proof, and inbound flow all compound together.

Why Pulseforge

Built by an operator.

Most AI agencies are run by engineers who've never operated a business. I'm the operator behind Pulseforge, with more than a decade spent running restaurants and a cleaning company. I know what it's like to have a no-show employee at 7am, to balance a Saturday floor, to make payroll work against a two-week-out invoice.

Service businesses don't need another piece of software. They need an agency that understands the margins, the seasonality, and the constant pull of operations that makes marketing impossible to focus on. That understanding is the entire reason Pulseforge exists, and it's why every decision, from the per-client send cap to the daily digest format to the cancel-anytime billing, looks the way it does.
