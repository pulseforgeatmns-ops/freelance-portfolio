01  ·  The business

A real operator in the Kanawha Valley.

Mountain State Home Innovations is the kind of business Pulseforge was built for. Brad and Dustin handle decks, siding, windows, roofing, emergency repairs — the work that keeps a West Virginia home livable through hurricane season and Appalachian winters. They're known in their market. Their reviews are strong. Their reputation is local.

What they didn't have was a system. Leads came in from referrals and Google Business Profile. Follow-up depended on whichever of them remembered. Months would pass between intentional marketing pushes. Like most service-business operators, they were busy running the work — not building the funnel that brings more of it in.

When they evaluated marketing options, the math made sense: a part-time marketing hire would cost $3,000–$5,000 per month and require management. Pulseforge would cost a fraction of that, run itself, and report back every morning. They signed a 90-day free trial.

02  ·  What we built

A seven-agent stack for the Kanawha Valley.

Onboarding started with a Letter of Authorization, an intake form covering their Ideal Customer Profile, and OAuth-based integrations for Gmail and Google Business Profile. From there, Pulseforge deployed seven agents against their account.

Scout sweeps the Charleston, Huntington, and broader Kanawha Valley markets nightly for homeowners and commercial property managers matching their target profile. Emmett sends a four-step cold sequence into warm-up, then maintains the pipeline. Riley reads every reply and routes the warm ones to Brad and Dustin the same day. Vera watches their Google Business Profile reviews. Max coordinates the whole stack — keeping every agent on schedule, escalating anomalies, making sure nothing falls through.

On top of that, Paige publishes weekly content across their channels, and Rex compiles a weekly digest covering what happened across the engagement. Seven agents, one client, fully live.

04  ·  What's next

From pipeline to closed work.

MSHI is in the early weeks of their 90-day trial. Scout has already identified more than a hundred prospects, Emmett is sending into the warm-up window, and Riley is monitoring for replies. As the response patterns accumulate over the coming weeks, we'll tune the sequence — refining language by vertical, tightening targeting, adjusting cadence.

Beyond the initial campaign, the roadmap includes activating Vera's Google Business Profile monitoring once API access is granted, expanding Scout into adjacent Appalachian markets, and converting the trial to recurring engagement at the 90-day mark. The longer goal: a fully self-sustaining outbound and content engine — one Brad and Dustin never have to manage.
